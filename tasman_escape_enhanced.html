Tasmania's Natural Wonders

Explore dramatic coastlines, pristine beaches, and rich history around Nubeena

üèÑ

Roaring Beach

Experience wild surf and pristine sand dunes. The only surfing beach on the west coast of Tasman Peninsula, perfect for sunset walks and watching surfers ride powerful swells. Access via scenic gravel road from Nubeena.

ü•æ

Cape Raoul

Hike through eucalypt forest to towering 300m dolerite cliffs. The 7km track in Tasman National Park offers sweeping ocean views and dramatic geological formations. One of Tasmania's most spectacular coastal walks.

White Beach

A sheltered 3km bay with calm, shallow waters ideal for families. Perfect for swimming, kayaking, and picnics. Features jetty, boat ramp, and views of Brother and Sister Islands. Very low hazard rating makes it perfect for all ages.

üèõÔ∏è

Port Arthur Historic Site

Step back in time at this UNESCO World Heritage site. Explore well-preserved convict ruins, immersive museum exhibits, and learn about Tasmania's fascinating colonial history through guided tours and ghost walks.

üåä

Tasman Arch

Marvel at nature's power at this soaring natural bridge carved by ocean waves. Walk to Devil's Kitchen, a dramatic collapsed sea cave. Easy access from car park with spectacular clifftop views.

Local Arts & Dining

Discover artists' studios, boutique galleries, and fresh Tasmanian produce. Sample local wines, craft beers, and seafood at nearby eateries. Experience authentic Tasmanian hospitality and creativity.
